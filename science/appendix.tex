\section{Mathematics}

Some terminology is reviewed in Section \ref{sec:terminology}.

\subsection{Terminology}\label{sec:terminology}

A \emph{curve} in a topological space $X$ is defined to be a continuous function from an interval in $\R$ to $X$.
A curve in a topological module is called \emph{nonvanishing} if it is nowhere equal to the additive identity $\vzero$.
For any curve $\vr(t)$ in a normed vector space, the \emph{radial function} of $\vr$ is the function $r$ defined pointwise by $r(t) = \left\|\vr(t)\right\|$.

\subsection{Fields of real and complex numbers}\label{sec:notation}

Denote the totally ordered field of real numbers by
\begin{align*}
	\left\langle \R, \R \times \R \xrightarrow{+} \R, 0, \R \times \R \xrightarrow{\cdot} \R, 1, \leq \right    \rangle
\end{align*}
and the field of complex numbers as follows.
\begin{align*}
	\left\langle \C, \C \times \C \xrightarrow{+} \C, 0, \C \times \C \xrightarrow{\cdot} \C, 1 \right\rangle
\end{align*}
