\section{Introduction}

Denote the totally ordered field of real numbers by
\begin{align}
	\left\langle \R, \R \times \R \xrightarrow{+} \R, 0, \R \times \R \xrightarrow{\cdot} \R, 1, \leq \right\rangle
\end{align}
and the field of complex numbers as follows.
\begin{align}
	\left\langle \C, \C \times \C \xrightarrow{+} \C, 0, \C \times \C \xrightarrow{\cdot} \C, 1 \right\rangle
\end{align}
The field $\C$ of complex numbers carries the involution $\C \xrightarrow{\overline{\bullet}} \C$ of complex conjugation and the complex inner product $\C \times \C \xrightarrow{\braket{\bullet \mid \star}} \C$ given by $\braket{z \mid w} = \overline{z} w$.
Denote the totally ordered rig of natural numbers by $\left\langle \N, \N \times \N \xrightarrow{+} \N, 0, \N \times \N \xrightarrow{\cdot} \N, 1, \leq \right\rangle$.
For any natural $n \in \N$, denote the real $n$-dimensional additive group as follows.
\begin{align}
	\left\langle \R^n, \R^n \times \R^n \xrightarrow{+} \R^n, \mbf{0} \right\rangle
\end{align}
